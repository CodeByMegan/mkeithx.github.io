Microsoft Teams is a powerful collaboration tool, but you might occasionally encounter issues. Here’s a quick guide to help you troubleshoot and resolve common problems.

:::tip[pro-tip]

The steps and guides in this article are for internal IT use and are meant to help with common Microsoft Teams problems. Always check the [official documentation](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoftteams/deploy-enterprise-setup?tabs=ChatTeamsChannels) for the latest information and full details.


:::




## Recommended settings

1. Go to Settings: Click on your profile picture or the three-dot menu in the upper-right corner, then select "Settings".

    ![close-ms-teams](/img/troubleshooting/ms-teams-settings.png)

2. Navigate to General: Go to the "General" tab.

    ![close-ms-teams](/img/troubleshooting/ms-teams-generalsettings.png)

    - ❌ Disable Auto-Start
    - ❌ Disable Open application in background
    - ❌ Disable On close, keep the application running
    - ✅ Enable Register the new Teams as the chat app for Microsoft 365 (requires restarting Microsoft 365 applications)

## 1. **Unable to Log In**

If you can’t log in, try the following: -->

- **Check Your Credentials:** Ensure you’re using the correct email and password.
- **Restart Teams:** Close the application completely and reopen it.
- **Clear the Cache:** For desktop users, clearing the Teams cache can help:
  - Exit Teams.
  - Navigate to `%appdata%\Microsoft\Teams` and delete all files in the folder.
